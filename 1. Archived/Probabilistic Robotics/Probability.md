Let $X$ be a random variable, and $x$ is a possible outcome of $X$. We denote the probability of $X$ having value $x$: (<u>1</u>)

$$p(X = x)$$

# Example

For example, flipping a coin have probability:

$$p(X = \text{"head"}) = p(X = \text{"tail"}) = \frac 1 2$$
